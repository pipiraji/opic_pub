은행



I will tell you about banks in Korea.

There are over 100 banks including commercial banks, mutual savings banks and specialized banks.

Among them the big four are Kookmin, Shinhan, Woori and Hana Bank.

We can easily find banks downtown. They are much like banks in other countries.

When I walk into a bank, the first thing I can see is the lobby area with some benches and magazines. I take a waiting number and wait for my turn there.

When my number is called, I go to a bank window and take care of my business with a teller. There are tellers sitting behind the windows.

Banks are open from 9 am to 4 pm Monday through Friday. This is about banks in Korea.




People go to banks to take care of various things.

First of all, I can open or close an account. Also, I go there to check my balance, deposit, transfer of withdraw money.

Before travelling abroad, I exchange foreign currency there.

Moreover, I can apply for a credit card or a loan.

Lastly, I can pay the bills or traffic tickets there.

I do many different things at the bank and the tellers are very friendly and helpful.




The way we bank has changed radically over the years.

If you wanted to transfer money, it meant a trip to a bank.

However, people don’t go to a bank anymore to do it.

We use online banking and mobile banking.

Most banks have their own app and offer the internet banking service, so I can do banking on my phone wherever I am, wherever I want to.

Internet banking can be used to manage our everyday banking, open accounts, transfer money and check the balance.

I think it is most remarkable changes regarding banking.
