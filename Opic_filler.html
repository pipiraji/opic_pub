Transitional Words

1. Addition (추가)


1. Additionally

- Meaning: In addition; as an extra or further consideration. (추가적으로; 또 다른 고려 사항으로)

- For example: The new model is faster. Additionally, it consumes less power.


2. Moreover

- Meaning: In addition to what has been said; furthermore. (앞서 말한 것에 더하여; 게다가)

- For example: The plan is expensive. Moreover, it lacks public support.


3. Furthermore

- Meaning: In addition; continuing from what has been said. (추가적으로; 앞서 언급된 것에 이어서)

- For example: The project will benefit the community. Furthermore, it will create jobs.


4. Not to mention

- Meaning: Used to introduce an additional point that reinforces the previous statement. (이전 진술을 강화하는 추가 사항을 도입할 때 사용)

- For example: The trip was expensive, not to mention the time it took to get there.


5. Also

- Meaning: In addition; too. (또한; 더불어)

- For example: She is a talented musician. She also paints beautifully.


6. On top of that

- Meaning: In addition to something; as well as that. (어떤 것에 더하여; 또한)

- For example: He was late for the meeting. On top of that, he forgot his notes.


7. Another point is

- Meaning: Used to introduce an additional argument or point. (추가적인 논점이나 사항을 소개할 때 사용)

- For example: We need to cut costs. Another point is that we should improve efficiency.


8. What’s more

- Meaning: Used to add further information that emphasizes the previous statement. (이전 진술을 강조하는 추가 정보를 덧붙일 때 사용)

- For example: The service was slow. What’s more, the food was cold.


9. As well as that

- Meaning: Used to introduce an additional fact or point. (추가적인 사실이나 사항을 도입할 때 사용)

- For example: The weather was terrible, as well as that, the roads were blocked.


10. In addition

- Meaning: Used to introduce something extra or further. (추가적인 내용을 도입할 때 사용)

- For example: The book is informative. In addition, it's well-written.


11. Besides

- Meaning: Apart from; in addition to. (~ 외에; 추가적으로)

- For example: I don’t feel like going out. Besides, I have a lot of work to do.


12. Plus

- Meaning: As well as; in addition. (또한; 추가적으로)

- For example: The new phone is cheaper. Plus, it has a better camera.


13. And another thing

- Meaning: Used to add another point or argument. (또 다른 점이나 주장을 덧붙일 때 사용)

- For example: The room is too small. And another thing, it's too noisy.


14. To add to this

- Meaning: Used to introduce additional information. (추가적인 정보를 도입할 때 사용)

- For example: The software is user-friendly. To add to this, it’s also very fast.


15. Along with

- Meaning: In addition to something; together with. (~와 함께; ~에 더하여)

- For example: She brought her laptop along with a notebook.


16. In the same vein

- Meaning: In a similar manner; in the same way. (비슷한 방식으로; 같은 맥락에서)

- For example: The first movie was a hit. In the same vein, the sequel was also successful.


17. Likewise

- Meaning: In the same way; similarly. (같은 방식으로; 비슷하게)

- For example: She offered her help. Likewise, her colleagues were also supportive.


2. Contrast (대조)

1. However

- Meaning: Used to introduce a statement that contrasts with or seems to contradict something that has been said previously. (그러나; 하지만)

- For example: The weather was cold; however, we still went hiking.


2. On the other hand

- Meaning: Used to introduce a contrasting point of view, fact, or situation. (다른 한편으로는; 반면에)

- For example: He loves chocolate. On the other hand, his brother dislikes it.


3. In contrast

- Meaning: Used to show a difference between two things. (대조적으로)

- For example: The south of the country is hot. In contrast, the north is very cold.


4. Conversely

- Meaning: Introducing a statement or idea which reverses one that has just been made or referred to. (반대로; 역으로)

- For example: He prefers a quiet evening at home. Conversely, she enjoys going out.


5. Instead

- Meaning: As an alternative or substitute. (대신에)

- For example: We didn’t go to the park; instead, we stayed home and watched a movie.


6. Nevertheless

- Meaning: In spite of that; despite what has just been said. (그럼에도 불구하고)

- For example: It was raining, but they went hiking nevertheless.


7. Nonetheless

- Meaning: In spite of that; nevertheless. (그럼에도 불구하고)

- For example: The journey was difficult, but it was worth it nonetheless.


8. Whereas

- Meaning: In contrast or comparison with the fact that. (~에 반하여; ~인데 반해)

- For example: He likes action movies, whereas his wife prefers comedies.


9. While

- Meaning: Used to contrast two different things or situations. (~하는 반면에)

- For example: She was studying while her friends were out partying.


10. Even though

- Meaning: Despite the fact that. (비록 ~일지라도)

- For example: Even though it was raining, they decided to go for a walk.


11. Although

- Meaning: In spite of the fact that; even though. (비록 ~이지만)

- For example: Although he was tired, he continued working late into the night.


12. Despite

- Meaning: Without being affected by; in spite of. (~에도 불구하고)

- For example: Despite the heavy traffic, we arrived on time.


13. In spite of

- Meaning: Despite; without being affected by. (~에도 불구하고)

- For example: She succeeded in spite of many obstacles.


14. On the contrary

- Meaning: Used to intensify a denial of what has just been implied or stated. (그와는 반대로)

- For example: He didn’t fail the test; on the contrary, he passed with flying colors.


15. Differing from

- Meaning: Being different or distinct from. (~와는 다르게)

- For example: Her opinion is differing from the majority of the group.


3. Comparison (비교)

1. Similarly

- Meaning: In a similar way; likewise. (유사하게; 마찬가지로)

- For example: She loves reading; similarly, her sister is always with a book.


2. In the same way

- Meaning: In a manner similar to something else. (같은 방식으로)

- For example: He completed the project in the same way as he did the last one.


3. Just as

- Meaning: In exactly the same way as. (마치 ~처럼)

- For example: Just as the sun rises in the east, so too does it set in the west.


4. Just like

- Meaning: In the same way as; similar to. (마치 ~처럼)

- For example: He dances just like a professional.


5. Compared to

- Meaning: In relation to something else; in contrast with. (~에 비해)

- For example: The new model is more efficient compared to the older version.


6. In comparison

- Meaning: When compared to something else. (비교해보면)

- For example: In comparison to last year, this year’s sales have increased.


7. Equally

- Meaning: To the same degree or level; similarly. (동등하게; 똑같이)

- For example: She is equally talented in both singing and dancing.


8. By the same token

- Meaning: For the same reason; in a similar way. (같은 이유로; 마찬가지로)

- For example: You shouldn’t trust him; by the same token, you shouldn’t trust his friends either.


9. Much like

- Meaning: Very similar to; in the same way as. (매우 ~와 같은)

- For example: The situation is much like what happened last year.


10. In a similar manner

- Meaning: In a way that is alike or comparable. (비슷한 방식으로)

- For example: The two artists work in a similar manner, focusing on realism.


11. Resembling

- Meaning: Looking or being similar to something else. (~을 닮은)

- For example: The sculpture is resembling a famous historical figure.


12. Correspondingly

- Meaning: In a way that is similar or analogous. (상응하여; 유사하게)

- For example: As their responsibilities increased, their salaries were raised correspondingly.


13. In parallel

- Meaning: At the same time and in a similar way. (동시에; 유사하게)

- For example: The two projects are progressing in parallel.


14. Likewise

- Meaning: In the same way; similarly. (같은 방식으로; 비슷하게)

- For example: She was very happy with the results; likewise, her team was thrilled.


15. Analogous to

- Meaning: Comparable in certain respects, typically in a way that makes clearer the nature of the things compared. (~에 유사한)

- For example: The structure of the human brain is analogous to a complex computer.


4. Cause and Effect (원인과 결과)

1. Because

- Meaning: For the reason that; since. (~때문에)

- For example: She stayed home because she was feeling ill.


2. Because of

- Meaning: Due to; on account of. (~때문에)

- For example: The event was canceled because of the bad weather.


3. Due to

- Meaning: Caused by; as a result of. (~로 인해)

- For example: The flight was delayed due to technical problems.


4. Owing to

- Meaning: Because of; on account of. (~때문에)

- For example: Owing to the heavy traffic, we were late.


5. Since

- Meaning: For the reason that; because. (~때문에)

- For example: Since it’s raining, the match will be postponed.


6. As

- Meaning: Because; for the reason that. (~때문에)

- For example: As he was late, the meeting started without him.


7. For this reason

- Meaning: Because of this; for this cause. (이러한 이유로)

- For example: She didn’t study hard. For this reason, she failed the exam.


8. On account of

- Meaning: Because of; due to. (~때문에)

- For example: The match was postponed on account of the rain.


9. Seeing that

- Meaning: Considering the fact that; because. (~을 고려하여)

- For example: Seeing that it’s already late, we should leave now.


10. Considering

- Meaning: Taking into account; in view of. (~을 고려할 때)

- For example: Considering the circumstances, he handled the situation well.


11. Therefore

- Meaning: For that reason; as a result. (따라서)

- For example: The team worked hard; therefore, they won the game.


12. As a result

- Meaning: Consequently; because of this. (그 결과)

- For example: The company cut costs; as a result, their profits increased.


13. Consequently

- Meaning: As a result; therefore. (그 결과)

- For example: She missed the deadline; consequently, her project was delayed.


14. Thus

- Meaning: As a result or consequence of this; therefore. (따라서)

- For example: The experiment failed; thus, the hypothesis was incorrect.


15. Hence

- Meaning: For this reason; consequently. (이 때문에)

- For example: He was the eldest son; hence, he inherited the title.


16. So

- Meaning: For that reason; therefore. (그래서)

- For example: It was late, so they decided to go home.


17. For this reason

- Meaning: Because of this; therefore. (이러한 이유로)

- For example: He was unwell. For this reason, he didn't attend the meeting.


18. Accordingly

- Meaning: As a result; in a way that is appropriate to the particular circumstances. (따라서)

- For example: She was promoted and was accordingly given more responsibilities.


19. This leads to

- Meaning: Causes or results in something. (이것이 ~로 이어지다)

- For example: Lack of sleep leads to decreased productivity.


20. As a consequence

- Meaning: As a result; therefore. (그 결과로)

- For example: The weather was terrible; as a consequence, the event was canceled.


5. Conclusion (결론)

1. In conclusion

- Meaning: Used to introduce a final statement or summary. (결론적으로)

- For example: In conclusion, the project was a great success.


2. To sum up

- Meaning: To summarize; to present a brief summary. (요약하자면)

- For example: To sum up, we achieved all our goals for the year.


3. In summary

- Meaning: Briefly stating the main points. (요약하면)

- For example: In summary, the study showed significant improvements.


4. To conclude

- Meaning: To bring something to an end; to summarize. (결론적으로)

- For example: To conclude, the evidence supports our hypothesis.


5. To summarize

- Meaning: To give a brief statement of the main points. (요약하자면)

- For example: To summarize, the results were better than expected.


6. All in all

- Meaning: Considering everything; on the whole. (전반적으로)

- For example: All in all, it was a successful event.


7. Ultimately

- Meaning: In the end; finally. (궁극적으로)

- For example: Ultimately, the decision rests with the board of directors.


8. Finally

- Meaning: At the end; after a long time. (마지막으로)

- For example: Finally, I would like to thank everyone for their hard work.


9. In the end

- Meaning: Ultimately; after everything has been considered. (결국)

- For example: In the end, the team managed to meet the deadline.


10. In short

- Meaning: Briefly; in a few words. (간단히 말해서)

- For example: In short, the plan needs more work.


11. On the whole

- Meaning: Considering everything; generally. (전체적으로)

- For example: On the whole, the project was a success.


12. As a final point

- Meaning: To introduce the last and usually the most important point. (마지막으로)

- For example: As a final point, we need to focus on customer satisfaction.


13. In brief

- Meaning: In short; briefly. (간단히 말해)

- For example: In brief, the findings confirm our initial hypothesis.


14. In essence

- Meaning: Essentially; in a basic or fundamental way. (본질적으로)

- For example: In essence, the problem lies in communication.


15. Overall

- Meaning: Taking everything into account; generally. (전반적으로)

- For example: Overall, the results were positive.


6. Illustration (예시)

1. For example

- Meaning: Used to introduce a specific instance or case to illustrate a point. (예를 들어)

- For example: You should eat more fruits, for example, apples and oranges.


2. For instance

- Meaning: Used to provide a specific example or case. (예를 들어)

- For example: Many cities have public parks, for instance, Central Park in New York.


3. Such as

- Meaning: Used to introduce specific examples. (~와 같은)

- For example: You can use many kinds of fruit, such as strawberries and blueberries, in this recipe.


4. Including

- Meaning: Used to introduce specific items or examples that are part of a larger group. (~을 포함하여)

- For example: The store sells various electronic gadgets, including laptops and smartphones.


5. Namely

- Meaning: Used to specify particular examples or to clarify what has been mentioned. (즉)

- For example: The project involves several tasks, namely research, design, and implementation.


6. To illustrate

- Meaning: Used to provide an example that explains or clarifies something. (예를 들어 설명하자면)

- For example: To illustrate the benefits of exercise, consider the case of improved mental health.


7. In particular

- Meaning: Used to single out one specific item or example. (특히)

- For example: I enjoy many types of music, in particular, jazz and classical.


8. Specifically

- Meaning: Used to provide a clear and exact example or detail. (구체적으로)

- For example: The company focuses on green energy, specifically solar and wind power.


9. As an example

- Meaning: Used to introduce a specific instance to clarify a point. (예를 들어)

- For example: Many animals can adapt to different climates. As an example, polar bears thrive in cold environments.


10. To give an example

- Meaning: Used to introduce a specific instance to clarify a statement. (예를 들면)

- For example: Some vegetables are high in vitamins. To give an example, spinach is rich in iron.


11. One example is

- Meaning: Used to introduce a specific instance or case to support a statement. (하나의 예로는)

- For example: Many countries have strict environmental laws. One example is Germany's recycling program.


12. Like

- Meaning: Used to introduce specific examples similar to what has been mentioned. (~와 같은)

- For example: Many fruits are high in vitamin C, like oranges and kiwis.


13. In the case of

- Meaning: Used to specify a particular instance or situation. (~의 경우에는)

- For example: In the case of a power outage, you should use a flashlight.


14. Among others

- Meaning: Used to indicate that there are additional items or examples not specifically mentioned. (~을 포함하여 여러 가지 중에서)

- For example: The company provides several services, including web development, graphic design, among others.


15. Especially

- Meaning: Used to single out one particular example that is more relevant or important. (특히)

- For example: I enjoy outdoor activities, especially hiking and camping.


Filler words or Expression 

1. Well, let me think about that for a moment.

- Meaning: Used to indicate that you need some time to consider or reflect on a question or topic. (음, 잠시 생각해 보겠습니다)

- For example: Well, let me think about that for a moment. It’s a complex issue.


2. That’s an interesting question.

- Meaning: Used to acknowledge that the question posed is engaging or thought-provoking. (흥미로운 질문이네요)

- For example: That’s an interesting question. I hadn’t considered it from that angle before.


3. Let me see…

- Meaning: Used to indicate that you are considering or searching for the answer. (음, 잠시만요…)

- For example: Let me see… I think the best way to approach this is to start with the basics.


4. How can I put this?

- Meaning: Used when you are trying to find the right words to explain something. (어떻게 표현할까요?)

- For example: How can I put this? It’s like trying to fit a square peg into a round hole.


5. What I mean is

- Meaning: Used to clarify or elaborate on what you’ve just said. (내 말은)

- For example: What I mean is that we need to consider all possible solutions before deciding.


6. Let me think

- Meaning: Used to signal that you are taking a moment to ponder or reflect on something. (생각해 보겠습니다)

- For example: Let me think for a second... I believe the best option is to go with the second proposal.


7. Hmm, that’s a tough one.

- Meaning: Used to acknowledge that the question or problem is challenging. (음, 그건 어려운 문제네요)

- For example: Hmm, that’s a tough one. I’ll need to think carefully about how to answer.


8. I guess what I’m trying to say is

- Meaning: Used to clarify or restate what you have been trying to express. (내가 말하려는 것은)

- For example: I guess what I’m trying to say is that we need more time to finalize the details.


9. In other words

- Meaning: Used to restate something in a different way to clarify or emphasize it. (다시 말해서)

- For example: We need to reduce costs. In other words, we have to cut down on unnecessary expenses.


10. What I’m trying to express is

- Meaning: Used to clarify or explain your intended meaning. (내가 표현하려는 것은)

- For example: What I’m trying to express is that the new policy will benefit everyone in the long run.


11. Let me put it this way...

- Meaning: Used to offer an alternative explanation or way of presenting something. (이렇게 표현해 보겠습니다)

- For example: Let me put it this way: If we don’t act now, we might miss a crucial opportunity.


12. Well, that’s something I haven’t thought about before

- Meaning: Used to indicate that the topic is new or unexpected. (음, 그건 전에 생각해 본 적이 없는 문제네요)

- For example: Well, that’s something I haven’t thought about before. Let me consider it further.


13. Hmm, let me consider that for a moment

- Meaning: Used to indicate that you need a moment to think about a new or challenging question. (음, 잠시 생각해 보겠습니다)

- For example: Hmm, let me consider that for a moment. I want to make sure I address it properly.


14. That’s a great question. Give me a second to think

- Meaning: Used to acknowledge the value of the question and request a brief moment to formulate an answer. (좋은 질문입니다. 잠시 생각해 보겠습니다)

- For example: That’s a great question. Give me a second to think about how best to respond.


15. I need a moment to gather my thoughts on that

- Meaning: Used to indicate that you require a brief period to organize your ideas before answering. (그 문제에 대해 생각을 정리할 시간이 필요합니다)

- For example: I need a moment to gather my thoughts on that. It’s a complex topic.


16. From my perspective

- Meaning: Used to introduce your personal viewpoint or opinion. (제 관점에서 보면)

- For example: From my perspective, the new policy will be beneficial for employee morale.


17. Personally I believe

- Meaning: Used to express your individual opinion or belief. (개인적으로 나는 믿습니다)

- For example: Personally, I believe that more investment in education will lead to long-term benefits.


18. In my experience

- Meaning: Used to introduce a statement based on your own personal experience. (제 경험에 비추어 보면)

- For example: In my experience, good communication is key to successful project management.


Adjectives describing a person

1. Kind

- Meaning: Showing a friendly, generous, and considerate nature. (친절한)

- For example: She is always kind to everyone she meets.


2. Considerate

- Meaning: Showing careful thought for the feelings and needs of others. (배려 깊은)

- For example: He is very considerate and always makes sure everyone is comfortable.


3. Friendly

- Meaning: Displaying a pleasant and supportive manner. (친근한)

- For example: Her friendly demeanor makes her easy to approach.


4. Honest

- Meaning: Truthful and straightforward; not lying or cheating. (정직한)

- For example: It’s important to be honest in all your relationships.


5. Diligent

- Meaning: Showing careful and persistent work or effort. (근면한)

- For example: She is diligent in her studies and always completes her assignments on time.


6. Thoughtful

- Meaning: Showing consideration for the needs of others; reflecting on ideas deeply. (사려 깊은)

- For example: He gave me a thoughtful gift that showed he really understands me.


7. Energetic

- Meaning: Showing or involving great activity or vitality. (에너지 넘치는)

- For example: The energetic team quickly finished the project ahead of schedule.


8. Funny

- Meaning: Causing laughter or amusement; humorous. (재미있는)

- For example: She has a funny sense of humor that always makes people laugh.


9. Gentle

- Meaning: Mild in temperament or behavior; kind and tender. (부드러운)

- For example: He has a gentle touch when handling animals.


10. Loyal

- Meaning: Showing firm and constant support or allegiance. (충성스러운)

- For example: A loyal friend is always there when you need them.


11. Understanding

- Meaning: Showing empathy and comprehension toward others. (이해심 있는)

- For example: She is very understanding and listens to others’ problems with empathy.


12. Collaborative

- Meaning: Involving or relating to working together with others. (협력적인)

- For example: The collaborative project was a success thanks to everyone’s combined efforts.


13. Positive

- Meaning: Having an optimistic attitude and outlook. (긍정적인)

- For example: Her positive attitude helps uplift everyone around her.


14. Outgoing

- Meaning: Friendly and socially confident; eager to engage with others. (외향적인)

- For example: He is very outgoing and enjoys meeting new people.


15. Good-natured

- Meaning: Having a pleasant, amiable, and easygoing disposition. (다정한)

- For example: Her good-natured personality makes her a joy to be around.


16. Reliable

- Meaning: Consistently good in quality or performance; dependable. (신뢰할 수 있는)

- For example: He is a reliable employee who always meets deadlines.


17. Responsible

- Meaning: Being accountable for one’s actions and duties. (책임감 있는)

- For example: She is responsible for managing the team’s schedule.


18. Warm-hearted

- Meaning: Showing kindness and affection; compassionate. (따뜻한 마음을 가진)

- For example: Her warm-hearted nature makes her very approachable.


19. Affectionate

- Meaning: Showing love and care through physical gestures or words. (애정 어린)

- For example: The dog is very affectionate and loves cuddling with its owner.


20. Amiable

- Meaning: Friendly and pleasant in manner. (상냥한)

- For example: His amiable nature makes him popular among his colleagues.


21. Compassionate

- Meaning: Showing sympathy and concern for others' suffering. (동정심이 많은)

- For example: She is compassionate and volunteers at the local shelter.


22. Approachable

- Meaning: Friendly and easy to talk to. (다가가기 쉬운)

- For example: The teacher is approachable and always willing to help students.


23. Enthusiastic

- Meaning: Showing intense and eager enjoyment or interest. (열정적인)

- For example: He is enthusiastic about learning new skills.


24. Cheerful

- Meaning: Noticeably happy and optimistic. (명랑한)

- For example: Her cheerful attitude brightens up the office every day.


25. Brave

- Meaning: Showing courage and determination in the face of danger or difficulty. (용감한)

- For example: She was brave in standing up for what she believed in.


26. Cautious

- Meaning: Being careful to avoid potential problems or dangers. (신중한)

- For example: He is cautious when making decisions to ensure he doesn’t overlook any risks.


27. Wise

- Meaning: Having experience, knowledge, and good judgment. (지혜로운)

- For example: Her wise advice helped me navigate through a difficult situation.


28. Humble

- Meaning: Having a modest view of one’s importance; not arrogant. (겸손한)

- For example: Despite his success, he remains humble and down-to-earth.


29. Open-minded

- Meaning: Willing to consider new ideas and perspectives. (개방적인)

- For example: An open-minded person is willing to listen to different viewpoints.


30. Polite

- Meaning: Showing good manners and respect for others. (공손한)

- For example: Being polite is essential for maintaining positive social interactions.


Passive Voice ( be+pp)

Expressions to memorize:

1. Be shocked at

- Meaning: To be very surprised or upset about something specific. (…에 충격을 받다)

- For example: She was shocked at the sudden change in policy.


2. Be shocked by

- Meaning: To be very surprised or upset due to the action or behavior of someone or something. (…에 의해 충격을 받다)

- For example: He was shocked by the news of the company's bankruptcy.


3. Be made of

- Meaning: To be composed of particular materials, often visible. (…로 만들어지다)

- For example: The sculpture is made of marble.


4. Be made from

- Meaning: To be created from raw materials, often resulting in a different final form. (…로 만들어지다)

- For example: The wine is made from grapes.


5. Be surprised at

- Meaning: To feel astonished or amazed about a particular fact or situation. (…에 놀라다)

- For example: I was surprised at how quickly the news spread.


6. Be surprised by

- Meaning: To feel astonished or amazed due to the actions or influence of something or someone. (…에 의해 놀라다)

- For example: She was surprised by his unexpected kindness.


7. Be disappointed at

- Meaning: To feel let down or dissatisfied about a specific event or occurrence. (…에 실망하다)

- For example: He was disappointed at the outcome of the match.


8. Be disappointed in

- Meaning: To feel let down or dissatisfied with a person or thing over time. (…에 실망하다)

- For example: She was disappointed in her friend’s behavior.


9. Be disappointed with

- Meaning: To feel let down or dissatisfied with a particular experience or thing. (…에 실망하다)

- For example: I was disappointed with the service at the restaurant.


10. Be embarrassed about

- Meaning: To feel self-conscious or uncomfortable due to something. (…에 대해 당황하다)

- For example: He was embarrassed about his mistake in the presentation.


11. Be embarrassed at

- Meaning: To feel self-conscious or uncomfortable due to a specific situation or action. (…에 대해 당황하다)

- For example: She was embarrassed at the way she reacted during the meeting.


12. Be annoyed about

- Meaning: To be irritated or bothered by a specific issue or situation. (…에 짜증나다)

- For example: He was annoyed about the constant delays.


13. Be annoyed at

- Meaning: To be irritated or bothered by a particular action or occurrence. (…에 짜증나다)

- For example: She was annoyed at the loud noise coming from the street.


14. Be annoyed with

- Meaning: To be irritated or bothered by a person or their behavior. (…에게 짜증나다)

- For example: I was annoyed with my colleague for missing the deadline.


15. Be frightened at

- Meaning: To feel fear in response to a specific event or occurrence. (…에 두려워하다)

- For example: She was frightened at the sight of the dark alley.


16. Be frightened of

- Meaning: To feel fear due to a particular thing or concept. (…을 두려워하다)

- For example: He is frightened of spiders.


17. Be delighted at

- Meaning: To be very pleased about a specific event or situation. (…에 기쁘다)

- For example: She was delighted at the surprise party thrown for her.


18. Be delighted with

- Meaning: To be very pleased with a particular thing or experience. (…에 기쁘다)

- For example: He was delighted with the results of his work.


19. Be pleased at

- Meaning: To feel satisfied or happy about a specific occurrence or result. (…에 만족하다)

- For example: I was pleased at the positive feedback from the team.


20. Be pleased with

- Meaning: To feel satisfied or happy with a particular object or experience. (…에 만족하다)

- For example: She was pleased with the new design of the website.


21. Be ashamed about

- Meaning: To feel embarrassed or guilty due to a specific action or situation. (…에 대해 부끄럽다)

- For example: He was ashamed about his poor performance in the competition.


22. Be ashamed at

- Meaning: To feel embarrassed due to a specific event or reaction. (…에 대해 부끄럽다)

- For example: She was ashamed at her outburst during the meeting.


23. Be ashamed of

- Meaning: To feel embarrassed or guilty due to one's own actions or characteristics. (…을 부끄러워하다)

- For example: He was ashamed of his behavior during the argument.


24. Be relieved at

- Meaning: To feel reassured after a particular event or piece of news. (…에 안도하다)

- For example: She was relieved at the news that her test results were normal.


25. Be relieved by

- Meaning: To feel reassured due to someone’s actions or intervention. (…에 의해 안도하다)

- For example: He was relieved by her comforting words.


26. Be tired of

- Meaning: To feel exhausted or fed up with something due to repetition or overexposure. (…에 지치다)

- For example: I am tired of waiting in long lines.


27. Be satisfied with

- Meaning: To feel content or pleased with something. (…에 만족하다)

- For example: She was satisfied with the outcome of the project.


28. Be based on

- Meaning: To be derived or founded on something. (…에 기반하다)

- For example: The theory is based on extensive research.


29. Be covered in

- Meaning: To be covered by a layer of something, often completely. (…으로 덮여 있다)

- For example: The book was covered in dust.


30. Be covered with

- Meaning: To be covered by something that is applied or added. (…으로 덮여 있다)

- For example: The cake was covered with frosting.


31. Be filled with

- Meaning: To be occupied or abundantly supplied with something. (…으로 가득 차 있다)

- For example: The basket was filled with fresh fruits.


32. Be known as

- Meaning: To be recognized or identified by a particular name or title. (…로 알려져 있다)

- For example: He is known as an expert in his field.


33. Be known to

- Meaning: To be recognized or acknowledged by a particular group or individual. (…에게 알려져 있다)

- For example: The method is known to be effective by many researchers.


34. Be known for

- Meaning: To be famous or recognized for a particular quality or feature. (…로 유명하다)

- For example: The city is known for its vibrant arts scene.


양식의 맨 아래




Preposition + Verb-ing



1. By + Ving

- Meaning: To achieve something through a particular method or means. (…함으로써)

- For example: You can improve your skills by practicing regularly.


2. Feel like Ving

- Meaning: To have a desire or inclination to do something. (…하고 싶은 기분이다)

- For example: I feel like going to the beach this weekend.


3. Be / Get used to Ving

- Meaning: To become accustomed to a particular activity or situation. (…에 익숙해지다)

- For example: She is used to working long hours.


4. Object to Ving

- Meaning: To express disapproval or opposition to a specific action. (…하는 것에 반대하다)

- For example: He objects to changing the company’s policies.


5. Be busy Ving

- Meaning: To be occupied with a particular activity. (…하느라 바쁘다)

- For example: She is busy preparing for her upcoming presentation.


6. Cannot help Ving

- Meaning: To be unable to stop oneself from doing something. (…하지 않을 수 없다)

- For example: I cannot help laughing at his jokes.


7. Have trouble Ving

- Meaning: To experience difficulty with a particular activity. (…하는 데 어려움을 겪다)

- For example: He has trouble understanding complex instructions.


8. Go Ving

- Meaning: To engage in an activity, usually for enjoyment or leisure. (…하러 가다)

- For example: We went hiking in the mountains.


9. Be used to Ving

- Meaning: To be accustomed to a particular activity or routine. (…하는 데 익숙하다)

- For example: She is used to waking up early for her job.


10. Mind Ving

- Meaning: To be bothered or inconvenienced by an activity. (…하는 것이 신경 쓰이다)

- For example: Do you mind closing the door?


11. On + Ving

- Meaning: Immediately after doing something. (…하자마자)

- For example: On finishing her meal, she went for a walk.


12. Look forward to Ving

- Meaning: To anticipate with excitement or eagerness. (…하기를 기대하다)

- For example: I look forward to meeting you next week.


13. When it comes to Ving

- Meaning: Regarding or in relation to a particular subject. (…에 관해서 말하자면)

- For example: When it comes to cooking, she is a real expert.


14. Apologise for Ving

- Meaning: To express regret or sorrow for an action. (…한 것에 대해 사과하다)

- For example: He apologised for being late to the meeting.


15. Be worth Ving

- Meaning: To be valuable or rewarding to do something. (…할 가치가 있다)

- For example: This book is worth reading.


16. It is no use Ving

- Meaning: Doing something will not achieve the desired result. (…해도 소용이 없다)

- For example: It is no use arguing with him; he won’t change his mind.


17. Spend 시간/돈 Ving

- Meaning: To use time or money for a particular activity. (시간/돈을 …하는 데 쓰다)

- For example: She spends a lot of time studying for her exams.


18. Be used to V

- Meaning: To be accustomed to doing something, usually in the past. (과거에 …하던 것이 익숙하다)

- For example: I am used to jogging every morning.


19. Used to V

- Meaning: Refers to a past habit or state that is no longer true. (예전에 …하곤 했다)

- For example: He used to live in New York.


20. What about Ving

- Meaning: To suggest or inquire about a particular activity. (…하는 건 어떻겠어?)

- For example: What about going to the park this weekend?




1. Where can I find ~

- Meaning: To ask about the location of something. (…를 어디에서 찾을 수 있나요?)

- For example: Where can I find a good restaurant around here?


2. How much does it cost?

- Meaning: To inquire about the price of something. (이것이 얼마인가요?)

- For example: How much does it cost to repair my car?


3. When will it start?

- Meaning: To ask about the starting time of an event or activity. (그것이 언제 시작되나요?)

- For example: When will the meeting start?


4. How long will S + V?

- Meaning: To ask about the duration of an activity or event. (S가 V하는 데 얼마나 걸리나요?)

- For example: How long will the flight to New York take?


5. Do you have other ~?

- Meaning: To ask if additional items or options are available. (다른 ~가 있나요?)

- For example: Do you have other sizes of this dress?


6. Is it possible to ~?

- Meaning: To inquire about the feasibility of doing something. (…하는 것이 가능한가요?)

- For example: Is it possible to get a refund for this ticket?


7. Could you tell me why/how S + V?

- Meaning: To request an explanation about the reason or method of something. (S가 V하는 이유나 방법을 말해줄 수 있나요?)

- For example: Could you tell me why the project was delayed?


8. Could help me ~

- Meaning: To ask if someone can assist with a specific task or need. (…하는 데 도움을 줄 수 있나요?)

- For example: Could you help me find a good lawyer?
